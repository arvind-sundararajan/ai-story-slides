Introduction

Hegelai built prompttools to solve LLM experimentation challenges.

Prompttools is a technical framework developed by hegelai, designed to facilitate experimentation and testing with large language models (LLMs) and vector databases. The primary problem it aims to solve is providing a structured approach to evaluating the performance of LLMs and vector databases in various scenarios, such as testing and experimenting with prompts. This is achieved through the use of specific classes and methods, including the OpenAIChatExperiment class, which allows users to define experiments with particular models, messages, and temperatures. The framework also includes methods for running and visualizing these experiments, making it easier to analyze and compare results.

The technical design of prompttools is centered around its use of LLMs and vector databases, which are fundamental components of its architecture. The framework is built to work with specific models, such as gpt-3. 5-turbo and gpt-4, and allows users to configure experiments with different temperatures. This level of control enables users to fine-tune their experiments and evaluate the performance of LLMs and vector databases in a more precise manner.

Furthermore, prompttools provides various interfaces for interacting with the framework, including code, notebooks, and a local playground, which cater to different user preferences and needs.

One of the key aspects of prompttools is its focus on providing a flexible and adaptable framework for experimentation. The use of classes and methods, such as OpenAIChatExperiment, allows users to define and run experiments in a programmatic way, making it easier to automate and reproduce results. Additionally, the framework's support for exporting results to various formats, including CSV, JSON, and MongoDB, facilitates further analysis and integration with other tools and systems. The ability to work with familiar interfaces, such as Jupyter notebooks and Streamlit, also makes it more accessible to users who are already familiar with these environments.

The target audience for prompttools appears to be developers and researchers who work with LLMs and vector databases, and are looking for a structured approach to testing and experimenting with these technologies. The framework's technical design and features suggest that it is intended for users who are already familiar with programming concepts and have a basic understanding of LLMs and vector databases. The use of specific models, temperatures, and interfaces also implies that the framework is designed for users who are interested in evaluating the performance of these technologies in a more precise and controlled manner.
